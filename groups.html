Research Groups

Four groups, one mission

CARAI advances explainable, trustworthy AI through four coordinated research laboratories — from foundational theory to real-world decision support.

01 · Theory 02 · Multimodality
 03 · In-context Learning 04 · Decision Support
01
Group 01 · Theory

Explainable AI Theory

Understanding, certifying, and automating the reasoning of deep models.

Objectives
  • Generating visual explanations of images and inferring causality through temporal analysis of videos.
  • Measuring the reliability of deep learning algorithms, studying adversarial attack algorithms, and building robust explainable models resilient to those attacks.
  • Video-based deep learning models for environmental adaptation and situation-change detection.
  • Automated machine learning (AutoML) to design optimal architectures that maximize learning performance.
  • Automated debugging techniques for deep learning.
02
Group 02 · Multimodality

Multimodal Learning & Detection

Recognition and detection across voice, vision, and diverse sensors.

Objectives
  • Recognizing commands and speakers from a user’s voice input in adverse real-world environments.
  • Real-time deep-learning processing of varied sensor data — extracting targets, detection/identification, and tracking at scale.
  • Robust models resistant to adversarial information, deriving associations and causal relations among data to generate evidence for target detection.
  • Individual- and group-level behavior recognition using multimodal sensor information.
03
Group 03 · In-context Learning

Few-shot Learning in the Wild

Learning and adapting where labeled data is scarce or absent.

Objectives
  • Intelligent recognition designed for environments with limited training data.
  • Active learning techniques for the efficient use of unclassified data.
  • Situation awareness via reinforcement learning to detect anomaly signs by aggregating uncertain real-world information.
  • Generative adversarial networks to create, synthesize, and augment training data for object and environment recognition.
04
Group 04 · Decision Support

Decision Making & Recommendation

Explainable decisions and recommendations grounded in real situations.

Objectives
  • Automatically generating sentences that summarize and report object and action information from videos of unfolding situations.
  • Decision-support AI that generates decision candidates and provides the reasons behind each recommendation.
  • Explainable decision selection based on result prediction.
  • Reinforcement-learning algorithms that recommend optimal decisions and explain why.
How the groups connect

Our four groups are not silos — they form a single, tightly-coupled research loop. Foundational theory from Group 1 hardens the models the other groups rely on, perception and learning advances flow between Groups 2 and 3, and every thread converges on Group 4 explainable decision-making. Each arrow below is a concrete hand-off of methods and results between labs.

Group 1 → others

Trustworthy models, robust learning, and model-reliability guarantees underpin every group.

Group 2 ↔ 3

Multimodal detection, audio & action understanding, and domain-adaptive learning flow both ways.

Group 3 → others

Few-shot data learning, detection, and data generation feed perception and decision-making.

Group 4 · convergence

Scene understanding and anomaly detection converge into explainable decisions and recommendation.
